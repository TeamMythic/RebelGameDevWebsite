How can I join the club?

Joining is easy! Simply attend one of our meetings, you can register as a member on the UNLV involvement center as well as our Discord. We welcome members of all skill levels.

What are the membership fees?

Our club is free to join! We believe that everyone should have access to game development resources and a supportive community.

What happens during meetings?

Our meetings are packed with opportunities to learn and grow. We offer tutorials on game development, host guest speakers who share real-world industry insights, organize game nights, and much more.

What opportunities are available for networking?

Our club frequently hosts guest speakers from the industry and participates in local game jams and hackathons. These events are great for meeting other developers and potential employers. Not only this, but our faculty advisory Dr. Sahl has helped many students get a career started in the gaming industry.

I have little to no experience. How can I contribute?

Everyone starts somewhere. With practice, you'll find that game development becomes easier and more intuitive over time. The key is to keep creating and learning.

How do you support beginners in game development?

We offer beginner-friendly workshops, tutorials, and one-on-one mentorship. Our community is very supportive, and we’re committed to helping you grow your skills.

Which game engine should I use?

Today, we’re fortunate to have a variety of game engines that handle much of the technical work for us. Each engine has its strengths and is suited to different types of games. The important thing is not just what the engine can do, but what you can achieve with it.

What is the Open Source Project?

Our club is currently in the beta phase of an exciting open-source project. This project is a collaborative framework where we work together on a unified project that includes multiple games. We're using Unity as our game engine, and GitHub as our repository. Click here to read the documentation or learn how to get started with the project on your machine.
